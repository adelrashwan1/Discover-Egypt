Ancient Egypt

The Great Pyramids

The last surviving wonder of the ancient world, the Pyramids of Giza have stood for over 4,500 years as a testament to Egyptian engineering.

Built as tombs for pharaohs, these structures align perfectly with celestial bodies and demonstrate advanced mathematical knowledge.

Learn More

Luxor Temple

Once known as Thebes, Luxor is often called the world's greatest open-air museum with its stunning temples and monuments.

The temple complex was dedicated to the Theban Triad of Amun, Mut, and Chons and was the center of the Opet Festival.

Learn More

Abu Simbel Temples

The twin temples of Abu Simbel were carved out of the mountainside during the reign of Pharaoh Ramesses II in the 13th century BCE.

These magnificent structures were relocated in the 1960s to avoid being submerged during the creation of Lake Nasser.

Learn More

Modern Egyptian Projects

New Administrative Capital

Egypt's new capital city is one of the largest ongoing development projects, designed to alleviate congestion in Cairo.

The smart city will feature government districts, business centers, and residential areas with modern infrastructure.

Learn More

Suez Canal Expansion

The expansion of the Suez Canal has doubled its capacity, allowing more ships to pass through this vital global trade route.

This mega-project included digging a new 35-kilometer channel and deepening and widening existing sections.

Learn More

Grand Egyptian Museum

Set to be the world's largest archaeological museum, the GEM will house an extensive collection of ancient Egyptian artifacts.

Located near the Giza pyramids, it will showcase the complete Tutankhamun collection for the first time.

Learn More

Natural Wonders of Egypt

The Nile River

The world's longest river, flowing through Egypt and creating a fertile green valley through the desert.

For millennia, the Nile's annual flooding deposited rich silt that made agriculture possible in this arid region.

Learn More

Red Sea Coral Reefs

Home to some of the world's most spectacular coral reefs and marine biodiversity.

The clear waters and vibrant marine life make this a premier destination for diving and snorkeling enthusiasts.

Learn More

White Desert

A surreal landscape of white chalk rock formations shaped by wind erosion over centuries.

Located in the Farafra depression, this unique natural wonder features mushroom-shaped rocks and stunning geological formations.

Learn More

Egyptian Cuisine

Koshari

Considered Egypt's national dish, koshari is a delicious vegetarian meal made from rice, lentils, chickpeas, and pasta.

Topped with a spicy tomato sauce and crispy fried onions, it's a flavorful and satisfying street food enjoyed by all Egyptians.

Learn More

Ful Medames

A staple breakfast dish made from fava beans cooked slowly and seasoned with garlic, lemon juice, and olive oil.

Typically served with pita bread, vegetables, and hard-boiled eggs, it's a nutritious start to the day enjoyed across Egypt.

Learn More

Molokhia

A traditional Egyptian soup made from the leaves of the jute plant, cooked with garlic and coriander.

This nutritious green soup is typically served with rice or bread and sometimes accompanied by rabbit or chicken.

Learn More
